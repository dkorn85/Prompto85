# Specialists

## A. Advertising / Commercial pro

### The hook is everything
First **1–2 seconds** must stop the scroll. Open on the most arresting frame — motion into frame, a pattern interrupt, a question, or the product hero. Never open on a logo or a slow fade.

### Structures by length
- **6–10s (social/bumper):** Hook → single payoff → logo+CTA. One idea only.
- **15s:** Hook (0–2s) → Problem/desire (2–6s) → Product as answer (6–11s) → CTA + logo (11–15s).
- **30s:** Hook → Problem → Agitate → Solution/demo → Proof/benefit → CTA. Classic AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action).
- **60s ("Superbowl"):** Cinematic cold-open → narrative build with emotional turn → brand reveal as climax → resonant tag. Spectacle + story, product earns the ending.

### Two register poles
- **Spectacle (Superbowl):** big format, anamorphic, crane/drone, scored, hero lighting, high production gloss. → blend "Epic Blockbuster" + "Hyperreal Commercial".
- **Authentic (UGC):** handheld, 35mm, available light, direct-to-camera, natural audio. → "Documentary Real". Higher trust, lower gloss.

### Frame craft for ads
- Hero shots: product centered or rule-of-thirds, soft high-key, glossy specular highlights, shallow DoF, slow orbit/push-in.
- Leave **clean negative space** for logo + CTA text (top or lower third).
- For GPT Image 2 ad stills: put CTA/headline in quotes, specify font/weight/color/placement, `render verbatim`.
- End card: brand mark + one-line tag + CTA; high contrast, legible at small size.

### Beat thinking → CUT
Write the ad as a master storyboard, one beat per panel, then CUT: Kling for the connected 15s spot (shot list), Seedance for individual hero beats, GPT Image 2 for the end card + key art.

## B. Action / movement pro

### Make motion read as real
- **Ground contact & weight transfer:** "lands heel-first, rolls forward, visible weight shift" — stops floating/moonwalking (works in Kling & Seedance).
- **Mass & impact:** describe the *consequence* — "the punch lands, head snaps back, sweat sprays", "boots skid, dust kicks up". Physics consequences sell force.
- **Cloth/hair/secondary motion:** "coat flaps", "hair whips", "smoke trails" — secondary motion = believability.

### Shot grammar for action
- **One beat per shot** (especially Seedance): wind-up / strike / aftermath are three panels, not one.
- **Impact frame:** hold the peak of force for a beat; in stills (GPT Image 2) freeze the decisive instant.
- **Speed:** "slow-motion, 120fps look" for impact; "real-time, urgent" for chase.
- **Coverage:** alternate wide-for-geography (where continuity allows) with CU-for-impact. Note: user's Kling convention forbids wide shots — stay tight and imply geography.
- **180° rule:** keep screen direction consistent across shots so the action stays readable.
- **Camera energy:** handheld/FPV for chaos, locked/Steadicam for clarity. ONE move per shot still applies.

### Escalation
Structure an action sequence as rising stakes: setup → first exchange → complication → climax (biggest impact frame) → resolution. Each panel raises intensity.

### Action templates
- Chase: `Dynamic FPV drone shot chasing [subject] through [environment], real-time urgency, motion blur on background, subject sharp, [palette]`.
- Combat beat: `[shot size], [attacker] [single strike] at [target], impact connects, [consequence: snap/spray/skid], [camera move], [light]`.
- Hero entrance: `low-angle [shot], [subject DNA] walks toward camera, slow push-in, coat moving, volumetric backlight, [palette]`.
