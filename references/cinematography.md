# Cinematography Reference

The professional vocabulary the engine uses to translate "a feeling" into camera, lens, light, and color terms the models obey. Pull exact terms from here into prompts.

## 1. Focal length & its psychology (full-frame equivalents)
- **14–24mm ultra-wide:** vast space, distortion at edges, immersive/uneasy. Environments, dream/scale, FPV.
- **28–35mm wide:** documentary, "you are there", lets environment read. Naturalistic dialogue, handheld realism (matches user's IMAX-handheld convention).
- **40–50mm normal:** ~human eye, neutral, honest. Default for grounded scenes.
- **85mm short tele / portrait:** flattering compression, separated subject, intimate. Beauty, hero faces.
- **100–135mm tele:** strong compression, isolated subject, flattened background. Tension, surveillance feel.
- **200mm+ long tele:** extreme compression, telephoto "stacking", voyeur/epic distance.
- **Macro:** texture, product detail, SEM-like extreme closeness (fits the Chazon "precision object" look).

## 2. Lens families & format
- **Spherical:** clean, modern, neutral bokeh.
- **Anamorphic:** 2.39:1 oval bokeh, horizontal blue flares, "cinema/epic". Say "anamorphic, horizontal lens flare, oval bokeh".
- **Vintage / uncoated:** soft contrast, blooming highlights, organic flares — nostalgic/dreamy.
- **Tilt-shift:** selective plane of focus, miniature effect.
- **Probe / macro:** insect-eye proximity.
- **Format cues:** "shot on 35mm film, fine grain", "65mm/IMAX large-format, high detail", "shot on ARRI Alexa, clean digital", "Super 8, heavy grain, gate weave".

## 3. Shot sizes (framing the subject)
ECU (extreme close-up: eyes/detail) · CU (close-up: face) · MCU (chest up) · MS (waist up) · MLS/Cowboy (knees up) · LS/Wide (full body + space) · EWS/Establishing (subject tiny in environment).
> User's Kling convention: **no wide shots** when continuity matters — stay ECU→MS.

## 4. Camera angles
Eye-level (neutral) · low angle (power, dominance) · high angle (vulnerability, smallness) · Dutch/canted (unease) · overhead/top-down (pattern, fate) · over-the-shoulder (relationship/POV) · POV (first person).

## 5. Camera movement (choose ONE per shot for Seedance/Kling)
- **Static / lock-off** — stillness, control.
- **Pan / tilt** — reveal, follow on a fixed pivot.
- **Dolly / track / trucking** — move with subject; push-in (intensify) / pull-out (reveal/abandon).
- **Pedestal** — straight up/down.
- **Crane / jib** — sweeping vertical scale.
- **Steadicam / gimbal** — smooth floating follow.
- **Handheld** — energy, realism, doc-feel (IMAX-handheld = controlled, weighty handheld).
- **Drone / FPV** — aerial sweep / aggressive chase ("dynamic FPV drone shot chasing…").
- **Orbit / arc** — rotate around subject (reveal, reverence).
- **Snorricam** — body-mounted, subject fixed while world moves (disorientation).
- **Whip pan / crash zoom** — punctuation, transition, comedy/action.

## 6. Lighting
- **Quality:** hard (sharp shadows, drama) vs soft (diffused, flattering).
- **Direction:** key (main), fill (shadow control), back/rim (separation), kicker, top, under.
- **Ratio:** high-key (bright, low contrast, ad/comedy) vs low-key (deep shadow, chiaroscuro, noir/sacred).
- **Portrait patterns:** Rembrandt (cheek triangle), butterfly/Paramount (glamour), split (half-face, tension), loop, broad/short.
- **Motivated / practical:** light that comes from a visible source in frame (lamp, window, neon, candle, screen) — reads as real.
- **Named looks:** "single-source hard key in darkness" (Chazon precision look) · "golden-hour backlight, long shadows" · "neon practicals, wet reflections" (neo-noir) · "soft north-window light" (Vermeer) · "volumetric god-rays through haze" (sacred).

## 7. Color
- **Temperature:** warm (3200K, intimacy) ↔ cool (5600K+, clinical/isolated).
- **Schemes:** teal-orange (blockbuster), complementary, analogous (harmony), monochromatic (control/elegance — Chazon near-monochrome), triadic.
- **Grading words:** "crushed blacks, filmic contrast", "lifted shadows, low-contrast pastel", "high-saturation pop", "desaturated steely palette", "bleach-bypass".

## 8. Composition
Rule of thirds · centered symmetry (formality/ritual) · leading lines · depth layering (fg/mg/bg) · negative space (isolation) · frame-within-frame · headroom/look-room · foreground occlusion (depth). For ads: leave clean negative space for logo/CTA.

## 9. Depth of field
Shallow (f/1.4–2.8: subject isolation, bokeh, intimacy) ↔ deep (f/8+: everything sharp, epic/landscape/product). State as "shallow depth of field, creamy bokeh" or "deep focus, edge-to-edge sharpness".

## 10. Translating a vibe → terms (quick map)
- "Epic / cinematic" → 65mm large-format, anamorphic flare, low-angle, crane, teal-orange, deep focus.
- "Intimate / emotional" → 85mm, soft key + rim, shallow DoF, slow push-in, warm grade.
- "Tense / thriller" → 100mm compression, low-key split light, handheld, desaturated, snorricam/whip pan.
- "Sacred / Chazon precision" → macro/normal, single hard source in black, near-monochrome, static lock-off, deep blacks, volumetric haze.
- "Hyperreal commercial" → 50–85mm, soft high-key, clean digital, glossy specular highlights, shallow DoF, slow orbit.
